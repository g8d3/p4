# Narration Script — Visual Style Research (ag-02)

## 0:00 - 0:15 | Introduction
Welcome to this visual style research session. I'm exploring the latest trends in AI video generation and visual styles for agent-created content. Let's dive into what's trending right now.

## 0:15 - 0:45 | AI Image Models
Looking at HuggingFace, we can see the top text-to-image models. Stable Diffusion v1.5 leads with over a million downloads. But the real story is FLUX.1 from Black Forest Labs — 587 thousand downloads for the dev variant, 220 thousand for schnell. It's Apache licensed, free for commercial use, and generates images in just 1 to 4 steps. That speed is crucial for agent workflows.

## 0:45 - 1:15 | Video Generation Landscape
The video generation space is exploding. ByteDance's Seedance 2.0 leads with realistic human faces at 1080p. Kling 3.0 from Kuaishou offers strong motion quality. Google's Veo 3.1 is highest quality but closed. OpenAI's Sora 2 remains premium but proprietary. The open-source alternatives are catching up fast.

## 1:15 - 1:45 | GitHub Trends
On GitHub, the Open-Generative-AI project has nearly 20 thousand stars — offering 200 plus models in a self-hosted MIT-licensed studio. Toonflow has 10 thousand stars, turning scripts into animated short dramas. The workflow pattern is consistent: script to LLM breakdown, storyboard, image generation with FLUX, video generation, TTS, then final composition.

## 1:45 - 2:15 | Visual Style Trends
Vertical format at 9:16 is now standard for TikTok, Reels, and Shorts. The Ken Burns effect — slow zoom and pan on static images — creates motion from nothing. Perfect for AI-generated stills. Color grading trends include muted pastels for dreamy aesthetics and high-contrast neon for cyberpunk vibes.

## 2:15 - 2:45 | Typography and Motion
Typography is shifting toward bold sans-serif headers with animated text reveals. Kinetic typography is trending — text that moves and transforms. Minimal text, maximum visual impact. Motion graphics templates from CapCut provide easy transitions and particle effects that signal the AI aesthetic.

## 2:45 - 3:15 | Agent-Created Video Specifics
For agents creating video, the strengths are clear: AI generates consistent themes, TTS adds personality, and screen recording captures authentic workflow. The weakness is static images feeling lifeless without motion effects. The opportunity is massive: FLUX plus Ken Burns equals rapid content creation, and the agent workflow itself IS the content — meta and authentic.

## 3:15 - 3:45 | Recommended Pipeline
The recommended pipeline for agent screencasts: record in vertical format, research live in the browser, type notes in the terminal for visibility, narrate findings with TTS, then compose with VAAPI for GPU-only encoding. The self-review loop with vision models improves quality each iteration.

## 3:45 - 4:00 | Conclusion
This research shows the AI video generation space is maturing rapidly. Open-source tools are catching up to proprietary ones. The key for agents is to leverage FLUX for images, Seedance or Kling for video, and build automated pipelines that scale through parallel discovery. Thanks for watching.
