# Open Source Voice AI Hits a Tipping Point

## Intro
Open source voice AI just hit a major milestone. Three groundbreaking tools have been released this month that together make the entire voice AI stack completely open source. Let's dive into what this means.

## Key Point 1: Gepard 1.0 - Streaming TTS Goes Open Source
First up is Gepard 1.0, a 600 million parameter streaming text-to-speech model released under Apache 2.0. What makes Gepard special is that it speaks as text arrives — no more waiting for the full sentence. It's built for real-time dialogue with incredibly low latency. This makes it perfect for voice assistants, live captioning, and interactive applications where natural conversation flow matters. And it's completely free and open source.

## Key Point 2: Voice Arena - The STT and TTS Leaderboard
The team behind Voice Arena launched voicearena.com, a public leaderboard that compares speech-to-text and text-to-speech models head to head. This is huge for developers trying to pick the right model for their use case. You can see word error rates, latency, and naturalness scores across dozens of models. It brings transparency and competition to the voice AI space.

## Key Point 3: LongCat-Avatar - Talking Videos from Photos
LongCat-Avatar is an open source tool that turns a single photo and an audio file into a talking video. It generates realistic lip sync and head movements. The implications are massive — content creators, educators, and businesses can now generate talking head videos without expensive studio equipment or proprietary software.

## Conclusion
Gepard for voice generation, Voice Arena for model comparison, and LongCat-Avatar for video creation. The full voice AI stack is now open, accessible, and ready for you to build with. The tipping point is here.
